Closely related to yellow jackets, hornets are the largest species of wasps in the world. They behave just like any other species of wasp, by building communal nests from wood pulp. These nests vary wildly in size depending on how well the colony is thriving in a particular area, and can be built virtually anywhere that provides enough shelter. In warmer climates, they can last all year round, but elsewhere they are abandoned during winter before being re-built the next year.
