What was the strange origin of sunglasses?

Sunglasses, now common throughout the world, were actually invented for a reason other than to protect the users eyes from the sun. While inuits have used sunglasses for millennia in order to protect their eyes from snow-blindness, these were only made from pieces of bone with slits cut in them, working by decreasing the eyes exposure to sunlight rather than decreasing its intensity.
