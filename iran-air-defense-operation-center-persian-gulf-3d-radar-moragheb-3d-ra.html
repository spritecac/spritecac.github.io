April 19, 2020 (Persian calendar 1399/1/31)

Iran’s Army commander unveiled two new homegrown strategic radar systems on Sunday.

The two Iranian radars, “Persian Gulf” (شاخاب پارس) and “Moraqeb” (Arabic means vigilant مراقب), were unveiled in a ceremony attended by Army Commander Major General Abdolrahim Mousavi سرلشگر عبدالرحيم موسوي and Commander of Army’s Air Defense Force Brigadier General Alireza Sabahifard سرتيپ عليرضا صباحي فرد.

Persian Gulf is a HF-Band long-range and strategic radar system with an operational range of over 800 kilometers. The three-dimensional phased array radar system can detect all types of conventional and stealth targets, including ballistic missiles.

Moraqeb is also a S-Band three-dimensional phased array radar with high precision and cutting-edge technologies. With a range of 400 kilometers, the new Iranian radar is capable of detecting aerial targets with high precision and resolution.

Moraqeb can identify various small targets, including stealth drones, at low and medium altitudes.

Developed under a joint project involving experts from the Army and domestic knowledge-based companies, Moraqeb has been manufactured as a tactical radar for long-range aerial protection.

Iran has in recent years made great headways in manufacturing a broad range of military equipment, including air defense systems that use cutting edge technologies.
