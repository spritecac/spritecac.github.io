THIS VIDEO IS A RECONSTRUCTION OF THE FOLLOWING SITUATION IN FLIGHT:

02-OCT-2021. A Spirit Airlines Airbus A320neo (A20N), registration N922NK, performing flight NKS3044 / NK3044 from Atlantic City International Airport, NJ (USA) to Fort Lauderdale International Airport, FL (USA) was accelerating for takeoff from RW 31 when the pilots rejected takeoff and reported a bird strike. The controller advised them that there was a fire coming from the right engine and pilots initiated the evacuation on the runway. As it was reported, no one was injured.

TEXT VERSION OF COMMUNICATIONS (Read if subtitles in video were fast):

NKS3044: Tower, Spirit Wings 3044, ready to go, hold short of RW 31.
TOWER: Spirit 3044, Atlantic City Tower, RW 31 cleared for take-off, maintain 7000
NKS3044: Alright, 31 cleared for take-off, maintain 7000, Spirit Wings 3044.
COMMENTS: As it was reported the aircraft rejected takeoff at low speed (60 knots) and stopped on the runway about 1800 feet/550 meters down the runway.
NKS3044: ATC, we had a bird strike in the right engine. Come to a stop on the runway.
TOWER: Spirit 3044, roger.
TOWER: Spirit 3044, do you need any assistance?
NKS3044: Yes, send a trucks, please.
TOWER: Roger.
COMMENTS: About 22 seconds later.
TOWER: Spirit 3044, there appears to be fire under your right engine.
NKS3044: Alright, we… Alright, we got that, Spirit Wings 3044. And please roll the trucks.
TOWER: Trucks are coming.
COMMENTS: About 1 minute 55 seconds later.
NKS3044: Tower, we’re evacuating.
TOWER: Roger.
COMMENTS: After that the aircraft was evacuated. As it was reported, no one was injured.
COMMENTS: That’s all. Don’t forget to LIKE and SUBSCRIBE. Thanks.
