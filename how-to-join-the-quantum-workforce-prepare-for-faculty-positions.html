Building quantum technology requires a lot of people with different backgrounds (physicists, electrical engineers, computer scientists, software engineers, chemists, …) and it can be overwhelming to consider a career in quantum science and information.
 
By watching this series of panels hosted in partnership with the Center for Quantum Networks Engineering Workforce Development, viewers will learn from students and researchers in the field what it is like to work in quantum science and get advice on navigating the various pathways. This and future panels in this series will be dedicated to share information on careers in the field of quantum science and information at all levels. 
 
For more information on everything quantum at Yale, visit quantuminstitute.yale.edu
