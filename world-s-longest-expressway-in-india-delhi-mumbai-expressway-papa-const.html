The Delhi–Mumbai Expressway is an under-construction 1,250 km long controlled-access highway connecting the national capital Delhi with India’s commercial capital Mumbai.Foundation stone of the project laid by union ministers Nitin Gadkari, Sushma Swaraj and Arun Jaitley on 8 March 2019. Under construction Vadodara Mumbai Expressway has been subsumed in this project. The total cost of the project is expected to be ₹1 lakh crore. It passes through the states of Haryana (80 km), Rajasthan (380 km), Madhya pradesh (370 km), Gujarat (300 km) and Maharashtra (120 km). This under construction expressway will be a 8-lane (4 lanes in each direction) greenfield-alignment route in backward areas, which will reduce the current 24 hours travel time to mere 12 hours. This expressway, along with Western Dedicated Freight Corridor will be a vital backbone of the Delhi–Mumbai Industrial Corridor.
