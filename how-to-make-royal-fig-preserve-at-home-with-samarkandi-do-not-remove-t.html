Interesting facts about figs
Fig is one of the ancient fruits. In 372 bc, Theophrastus, the father of botany described hundreds of types of figs in his works.
Figs are consumed raw, used for pies, tartes, desserts, made marmalades, chutneys and sauces out of them. Figs are not recommended for people with diabetes and gout.
Figs are rich with vitamins, microelements, proteins and carbohydrates. It is especially high in potassium (which is necessary for the work of the heart and blood vessels) and iron (which helps to prevent anemia).
