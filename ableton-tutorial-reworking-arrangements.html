Ever feel like your arrangements just aren’t quite right?⁠
⁠
I’m sure you’ve had this feeling before… ⁠
⁠
You finish your track, and as you listen back to it, your head starts filling with ideas about how you can improve the final arrangement!
⁠
You open up your project, but the task to re-arrange everything seems arduous, complicated and messy.
Well here’s a great solution for you…
⁠
Here’s my quick and easy way to experiment with your track’s arrangement in a way that is fun, intuitive, and doesn’t eat into your studio time.⁠
⁠
I love this technique because typically it’s hard to re-arrange a track into a bunch of different ideas because of all the moving parts, lots of clips, lane after lane of automation and so on, but this helps you quickly come up with new ideas without destroying your original work.⁠
⁠
Plus now you know that it’s this easy to experiment, it takes the pressure off having to make your arrangement perfect the first time round, helping you to create more freely!⁠
